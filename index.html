The Problem

The system is a wall.
We take it down
one door at a time.

Three groups of people — all failing each other — because the tools don't exist yet.

01

People in Crisis

People in recovery, exiting incarceration, or facing homelessness don't know what help exists or how to access it. The system is fragmented, cold, and designed by people who've never needed to use it.

02

Overwhelmed Case Workers

Social workers and case managers can't keep up with the shifting landscape of benefits and services. They want to help more — they simply lack the tools to do it consistently at scale.

03

Broken Systems

Existing resources — BenefitsCal, agency websites, 211 — are fragmented and inaccessible. Critical information is hard to find, harder to understand, and nearly impossible to act on alone.

How It Works

Three ways in.
No wrong door.

Every person arrives differently. The platform meets them where they are.

I

Conversational

Just talk. No forms, no checkboxes. AI listens, responds with compassion, and maps needs from dialogue. Voice input supported for low-literacy users.

II

Guided

One plain-language question at a time. Clear, calm progression for users who find open dialogue overwhelming — but aren't ready for a long checklist.

III

Self-Directed Checklist

For people who know what they need. Full control of pace and sequence. Switch between any mode at any time — no lost progress, no penalty.

What We Believe

Twelve values.
One conviction.

Every person has worth.

Not earned. Not conditional. You have it because you exist. Every screen, every word reflects that back.

Everyone deserves a chance.

Not a handout — a chance. Dignity, opportunity, and hope aren't luxuries. They're rights.

Simplicity is respect.

Every unnecessary word, every confusing step we eliminate is an act of respect. Complexity is the enemy. Clarity is the goal.

Elegance matters.

The people who use this product deserve beautiful things, not just functional ones. How something feels is part of what it does.

Walk alongside. Don't carry.

We remove friction. We open doors. The journey belongs to the person making it.

We hire the people we serve.

Lived experience is a credential. No training manual replicates it. This is core business strategy — and mission outcome.

Earned trust. Always.

Trust is demonstrated through transparency and consistency. Every time. No exceptions.

The long game.

We build for the decade, not the quarter. Sustainable impact over fast growth. Always.

Our Story

Built by someone who lived it.

Valera Roots exists because getting help shouldn't be harder than the problem that brought you there. The founder entered detox in Los Angeles with no roadmap — figuring out SNAP, Medi-Cal, housing, and a dozen other systems alone, while still in crisis. He became the unofficial guide for other clients.


He promised his therapist a plain-language guide for future clients. Ten months later, on pension day, that promise became Valera Roots. The name came from Tati — who named his basil plant, and gave him a definition he needed: "the strong one, the healthy one, the one who thrives."
